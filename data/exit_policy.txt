Title: Employee Exit Policy

1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to provide a standardized and transparent process for managing employee exits, ensuring business continuity, protection of company assets, and a respectful experience for departing employees.

2. Scope
This policy applies to all permanent, temporary, and contract employees of Company XYZ whose employment ends through resignation, retirement, contract completion, layoff, or termination.

3. Types of Separation
- Voluntary Exit: Initiated by the employee, such as resignation or retirement.
- Involuntary Exit: Initiated by the company, including performance‑based termination, redundancy, or misconduct.

4. Notice Period
- Standard notice periods are defined in employment contracts or local labor law.
- Employees are expected to submit resignation in writing via the designated HR system or email.
- Management may waive or adjust the notice period based on business needs and applicable regulations.

5. Exit Process Overview
- Initiation: HR receives and acknowledges the exit request or notifies the employee in case of involuntary exit.
- Planning: Manager and HR agree on last working day, knowledge transfer activities, and handover owners.
- Execution: The employee completes handover tasks, returns company assets, and participates in the exit interview.
- Closure: HR processes final settlement, updates records, and revokes system access.

6. Handover and Knowledge Transfer
- The departing employee must document ongoing work, critical processes, and key contacts.
- A successor or designated colleague must be identified to receive the handover.
- The manager is responsible for verifying that critical tasks and information have been transferred.

7. Return of Company Assets
- Before the last working day, the employee must return all company property, including laptops, ID cards, access tokens, phones, and documents.
- The IT and Facilities teams must confirm that returned assets are in acceptable condition and update the asset register.
- Any unreturned or damaged assets may be subject to recovery as permitted by law and company policy.

8. Access Revocation and Data Security
- IT must revoke logical access (accounts, VPN, email, applications) on or immediately after the last working day.
- Shared credentials must be changed where applicable.
- Personal data belonging to the employee will be handled in line with privacy and retention policies.

9. Exit Interview
- HR should offer an exit interview to understand reasons for leaving and gather feedback on the work environment, leadership, and processes.
- Feedback may be summarized and shared in anonymized form with management for improvement actions.

10. Final Settlement
- HR and Payroll will ensure timely settlement of outstanding salary, benefits, incentives, and reimbursable expenses, aligned with local law.
- Deductions related to advances, loans, or unreturned assets must be clearly documented and communicated to the employee.
- Relieving letters and experience certificates will be issued in accordance with company standards.

11. Confidentiality and Post‑Exit Obligations
- Employees remain bound by confidentiality, data protection, and non‑disclosure clauses after exit, as per their contracts.
- Any non‑compete or non‑solicitation obligations must be clearly communicated during the exit process.

12. Policy Compliance
- Managers, HR, and employees are responsible for following this policy and related procedures.
- Deviations must be approved by HR and documented.
- This policy will be reviewed at least annually and updated to reflect changes in business, law, or best practices.
