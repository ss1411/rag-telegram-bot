Title: Information Security Policy

1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of Company XYZ’s information assets from threats such as unauthorized access, misuse, disclosure, alteration, and destruction.

2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, interns, third‑party vendors, and any other individuals who access Company XYZ information, systems, or facilities, regardless of location.

3. Information Security Objectives
Company XYZ will:
- Ensure that only authorized individuals have access to information and systems.
- Maintain the accuracy and completeness of information and system configurations.
- Ensure that information and systems are available to authorized users when needed.

4. Roles and Responsibilities
- Management is responsible for providing direction, resources, and support for information security.
- The Information Security team defines controls, monitors compliance, and manages security incidents.
- Employees and contractors must follow this policy, complete security training, and promptly report suspected security issues.

5. Data Classification
All information must be classified into one of the following categories:
- Public: Information that may be disclosed outside the organization without risk.
- Internal: Information intended for employees and trusted partners, not for public release.
- Confidential: Sensitive information whose unauthorized disclosure could cause harm to the organization, customers, or employees.

6. Access Control
- User access must follow the principle of least privilege.
- Unique user IDs are required for individual accountability.
- Access rights must be approved, documented, and reviewed periodically.
- Accounts must be disabled promptly upon role change or termination.

7. Acceptable Use
- Company systems and networks must be used primarily for business purposes.
- Users must not install unauthorized software, bypass security controls, or share passwords.
- Downloading, storing, or transmitting illegal content is strictly prohibited.

8. Network and System Security
- Endpoints must use up‑to‑date anti‑malware software and security patches.
- Administrative access must use strong authentication and secure channels.
- Remote access must be protected by VPN or other approved secure methods.

9. Incident Management
- All suspected or actual security incidents must be reported immediately to the Information Security team via the designated channel.
- The Information Security team will investigate, contain, eradicate, and coordinate recovery steps.
- Lessons learned will be documented and used to improve controls.

10. Training and Awareness
- All users must complete information security awareness training at onboarding and at least annually.
- Additional role‑based training will be provided for high‑risk roles such as administrators and developers.

11. Policy Compliance
- Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, contract cancellation, or legal action.
- This policy will be reviewed at least annually or when significant changes occur in the business, technology, or regulatory environment.
