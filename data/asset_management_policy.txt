Title: Asset Management Policy

1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure that all Company XYZ information assets, including hardware, software, data, and related services, are identified, recorded, protected, and managed throughout their lifecycle.

2. Scope
This policy applies to all information assets owned, leased, or otherwise managed by Company XYZ, and to all employees, contractors, and third‑party service providers who use or manage these assets.

3. Definitions
- Asset: Any item of value to the organization, such as laptops, servers, network devices, applications, licenses, and data.
- Asset Owner: The individual or role responsible for the lifecycle, protection, and proper use of a specific asset.

4. Asset Inventory
- An up‑to‑date inventory of all critical information assets must be maintained in a centralized register.
- The inventory must record at least: asset ID, description, owner, location, classification, and lifecycle status.
- New assets must be added to the inventory as part of the procurement or onboarding process.

5. Ownership and Responsibilities
- Each asset must have an assigned owner who is accountable for its classification, appropriate use, and periodic review.
- Asset owners must ensure that controls aligned with the asset’s classification are implemented and monitored.
- Users are responsible for protecting assets entrusted to them and reporting loss, damage, or theft immediately.

6. Asset Classification
- Assets must be classified according to their business value, sensitivity, and criticality.
- At a minimum, the following levels are used: Public, Internal, and Confidential.
- Classification determines the required security controls, such as encryption, access restrictions, and backup frequency.

7. Acceptable Use of Assets
- Assets must be used only for authorized business purposes unless explicitly permitted by management.
- Users must not modify asset configurations in ways that bypass security controls.
- Company software licenses must be used in accordance with vendor terms, and unauthorized software installations are prohibited.

8. Asset Lifecycle Management
- Acquisition: All new assets must follow approved procurement and security review processes.
- Deployment: Configuration standards must be applied before assets are placed into production.
- Maintenance: Assets must receive regular updates, patches, and health checks.
- Transfer: Movement of assets between users or locations must be recorded in the asset register.
- Disposal: Assets must be securely wiped or destroyed at end of life, with records retained for audit purposes.

9. Physical Security of Assets
- Portable devices (such as laptops and mobile phones) must be stored securely when unattended.
- Access to server rooms and network equipment must be restricted to authorized personnel only.
- Visitors must be escorted in restricted areas where critical assets are located.

10. Monitoring and Audit
- Periodic audits will be conducted to verify the accuracy of the asset register and compliance with this policy.
- Discrepancies must be investigated, and corrective actions documented.

11. Policy Compliance
- Non‑compliance with this policy may result in disciplinary action and, where applicable, legal consequences.
- This policy will be reviewed at least annually or whenever there are significant changes to the organization’s asset landscape.
